What is Political Risk?

Defining Political Risk

Download White Paper

Political risk is seen as the influence political change by any political actor may have on investments. For example this could be the President of say the US who introduces a new sanction on european imports. Or a political party promoting behaviour which reduces the certainty of justice. Classicaly this would mean when a politician takes influence on the business environment of his/her country in a positive/negative fashion. However With the creation of our masterthesis the passion for political risk rose tremendously. Our mission was to create an index, which comes form the people and represents the peoples uncertainty about a countries political situation, regulation and sanctions. All of these we wanted to create with a newly approached tool, which all of us are using every day: GOOGLE.™ With the data provision of Google Trends™ this became reality. We are in the beginning of our development phase and want to provide a solution for small, middle and big companies to monitor and forecast political risk in certain countries in many different time periods (yearly, monthly, daily and even hourly) at the expanse of very little costs. It was important for us to create a plitical risk tool, which can be used from everyone and can be adapted in any given way. For more information about our approach of measuring political risks download our white paper.

Important Political Risk Catalysts

National & International Actors, Institutional Strength & Culture

National Actors

Typically political events are triggered by high ranking politicians. They clearly have the strongest link to politic However, the introduction of social media has made it much easier for individuals or political groups to take political action. This shift in the political influence landscape makes understanding and forecasting risks much more nuanced.

International Actors

On top of the classical and new political national actors international infleunce has become a recurring topic in political risk. International activists and terrorist groups can have major influence on the political risks a country witnesses. "more in blog on"

Institutional Strength

Individual Actors can always influence a political system, however the institutional strength sets the boundries of how effectively they push the boundries. Steady institutions are a main factor for longterm stability. They are however, no guarantee.

Accounting for all Political Risk Catalysts

Is political risk not too complex to put in numbers?

Political risk inducers can come from pretty much any corner of the globe. How can we detect how high the risk for a political event really is?
The first link is from political risk to uncertainty. Which is actually just a wording issue. Risk as defined by Frank Knight in 1921 states clearly that political risk is an uncertainty, since risks only capture clearly defined outcomes.
We then start by proxying political risk with the percieved uncertainty of the population. When individuals feel uncertain about a topic lets say buying a house they will tend to try and reduce this uncertainty by improving their knowledge. Knowledge improves the understanding of the situation and the outcome of, in this example, buying a house will more likely meet their expectations. Where to better search for information than Google - the information codex. The more the population searches for a topic related to political risk the higher the percieved political uncertainty. The population is most likely to be under the influence of all uncertainty catalysts as their everyday lives revolve around navigating any type of uncertainty.
